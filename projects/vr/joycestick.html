Joycestick

Overview

Joycestick is reimagining James Joyce's Ulysses in Virtual Reality. The project is under active development by a class of of 30 students from Boston College, MIT, Northeastern and Berklee School of Music. As the teaching assistant of the course developing Joycestick, I had the plan and vision for this project over a year prior to the first time the class met. Once development began, I served as the Engineering Lead and shipped both the MVP and second version of Joycestick. Since I graduated (in Dec. 2016), I passed on my role [to someone smarter than me] to see the vision I have for this project through.

_

Why Ulysses?

My research advisor at Boston College in Digital Humanities, Prof. Joe Nugent is a Joyce Scholar, and as such, I've had an interest in bringing the intricate world of Ulysses to a medium that holds so much promise for telling complex stories. Furthermore, Ulysses is very much so about a story about experience and imagery– all aspects that make the story an excellent candidate for an unorthodox medium like VR. The current direction that Joycestick is heading breaks the story up logically by chapter and represents each through objects and images. These are translated into scenes that vary in degree of interactivity, and as the player through the scenes, an aspect of the story is uncovered and they feel rewarded.

Developing Joycestick

Working on Joycestick at "Reality, Virtually" Hackathon at the Media Lab at MIT.

The team of software developers for Joycestick comes from a diverse background. We have experience building applications for mobile, web, analytics and even embeded systems. But naturally, no one (with the exception of myself) had ever used Unity or built a video game. It was clear we had our work cut out for us and building the early versions of the game relied on our flexibiity as team, and being unafraid to scrap our progress at any time in favor of a more scalable and extensible approach.

Early on, this happened a number of times. As we began to explore a more well-organized code structure and object-oriented game architechture through better design patterns, branching off in a new direction became essential.  Some early Joycestick C# scripts include the one below defining the Class JoyceObject.
